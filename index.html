Career Stats

Mahendra Singh Dhoni was born 7 July 1981 in Ranchi,Bihar(Jharkhand) is a former Indian cricketer He was captain of the Indian national team in limited-overs formats from 2007 to 2017 and in Test cricket from 2008 to 2014. He plays as a right-handed wicket-keeper-batsman and is also the current captain of Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League.
              Dhoni's performance in the 2002-03 season included three half-centuries in the Ranji TrophyDhoni made his ODI debut on 23 December 2004, against Bangladesh in Chittagong and played his first Test a year later against Sri Lanka.He played his first T20I also a year later against South Africa.In 2007, he took over the ODI captaincy from Rahul Dravid and he also selected as T20I captain of India in this year.In 2008, he was selected as Test captain.His captaincy record in Tests format was mixed, successfully leading India to a series win against New Zealand in 2008 and the Border-Gavaskar Trophy (home series in 2010 and 2013) against Australia.
              He is known as the Official Father Of ICC tropies.Dhoni is the first cricketer to be a winning captain of all three major ICC trophies, as under his captaincy,India won the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2011 Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy, He also led India to victory in the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cup.Under his leadership, India won the 2010 and 2011 ICC Test Mace and 2013 ICC ODI Championship. He has scored 17,266 runs in International Cricket

MS Dhoni Legacy : The Man,The Myth,The Legend.

MS Dhoni's legacy in the world of cricket is far from small; it is immense and far-reaching. Here are some key aspects of his legacy: Captain Cool,MS Dhoni is often referred to as "Captain Cool" for his calm and composed demeanor on the field. Under his leadership, the Indian cricket team achieved numerous milestones, including winning the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup in 2007 and the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011. His leadership style and ability to handle high-pressure situations have left a lasting impression on the cricketing world. Exceptional Captainy,Dhoni is regarded as one of the greatest cricket captains of all time. His tactical acumen, innovative strategies, and ability to bring out the best in his players made him a legendary captain. He led India to the number one ranking in Test cricket for the first time in December 2009.Helicopter Shot,Dhoni's unique batting style, particularly his signature "helicopter shot," became iconic in cricket. He was known for his ability to finish matches with powerful hitting in the death overs. Wicketkeeping Skills: Dhoni was not only a prolific batsman and captain but also an exceptional wicketkeeper. His lightning-fast stumping and accurate glovework behind the stumps set new standards for wicketkeepers in the modern era. Mentorship and Leadership Off the Field, After retiring from international cricket, Dhoni continued to be a mentor and leader in various roles. He captained the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) in the Indian Premier League (IPL), leading them to multiple championships. His influence on young cricketers and his contributions to nurturing talent have been significant.Humble and Relatable Personality, Despite achieving immense success, Dhoni remained humble and relatable. His down-to-earth nature and the respect he showed towards the game, teammates, and opponents endeared him to fans worldwide. Cricketing Inspiration,Dhoni's journey from a small town to the pinnacle of cricket has inspired countless aspiring cricketers, particularly those from non-metro cities in India. He demonstrated that talent and hard work can take you far in the sport.
